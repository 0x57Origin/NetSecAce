Lesson 1.3 ‚Äî Key Terms Every Beginner Must Know

Threat ‚Äî The source of danger

Anything that has the potential to cause harm. Could be a hacker, a natural disaster, a disgruntled employee, or even human error.

Example: A hacker group targeting hospitals, a hurricane, malware
Vulnerability ‚Äî The weakness

A weakness or flaw in a system, process, or person that could be exploited. Threats exploit vulnerabilities.

Example: Unpatched software, weak passwords, open ports, untrained employees
Risk ‚Äî The probability of harm

The potential for loss when a threat exploits a vulnerability. Risk = Threat √ó Vulnerability

Example: Risk of a breach = hackers exist (threat) √ó unpatched server (vulnerability)
Exploit ‚Äî The actual attack

The code, technique, or tool used to take advantage of a vulnerability. A vulnerability exists ‚Äî an exploit turns it into a real attack.

Example: EternalBlue exploit used by WannaCry to attack unpatched Windows systems
Asset ‚Äî What we're protecting

Anything of value worth protecting. This includes data, hardware, software, people, and reputation.

Example: Customer database, company servers, employee credentials, source code
Control ‚Äî The safeguard

Any safeguard, policy, or countermeasure that reduces risk. Controls can be technical, physical, or administrative.

Example: Firewall (technical), security guard (physical), password policy (administrative)

üîó How They Connect

A threat exploits a vulnerability in an asset to create risk. An exploit is how the attack happens. A control reduces the risk.
